🎤 專題演講 (Keynotes)

Prof. Katia Sycara

Edward Fredkin Research Professor of Robotics, School of Computer Science, CMU
Talk Title: Long Horizon Prediction
"In this talk we present our work on predicting long-horizon sequence of actions from short-observation contexts. In a demonstration with a robotic manipulator, we show that predicting human intention in this way leads to effective collaboration between humans and robots."

Prof. Michael Lewis

Professor, School of Computing and Information, University of Pittsburgh
Talk Title: Theory of Mind for Coordination in Search & Rescue
"Theory of Mind entails predicting the evolving beliefs of other actors often under conditions of partial observability. Predicting the actions of others based on what you believe they believe to be true rather than your own beliefs can improve accuracy and therefore coordination. We describe three studies in which team performance is improved by incorporating Theory of Mind. In the first, human agent teams perform a bomb defusal task... In the second study LLMs coordinate to perform the task... In the third study OverCooked players infer the roles of their teammates..."
